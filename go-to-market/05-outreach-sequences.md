## 0. How To Use This Document

### 0.1 The one rule that governs every message

You are a founder asking finance leaders to **co-build** a product, not a rep selling software. The Databricks Early Access, Scale AI Design Partner, and Palantir Forward-Deployed motions we model after all share one trait: the first contact offers the prospect *influence and a problem solved*, not a demo of a finished thing. Every message in this library is curious, specific, short, and human. If a draft you write sounds like a templated SaaS sequence, delete it.

### 0.2 The non-negotiables (apply to all six sequences)

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Lead with a **trigger** you actually observed (`[TRIGGER]`) | Lead with "I wanted to reach out" or "Hope you're well" |
| Reference one **specific finance problem** (forecast accuracy, variance explained, vendor spend visibility) | List five capabilities in one breath |
| Keep cold messages **under ~75 words**, email under ~120 | Write paragraphs; attach a deck on message 1 |
| Ask for a **conversation**, not a meeting "to show you our platform" | Ask for "30 minutes to demo" |
| Say "design partner" and mean it — co-build, roadmap influence, founder access | Promise features that are roadmap, not live (see 0.4) |
| Sign as the founder, by name | Sign "The Sin City Analytics Team" |
| Stop after the cadence ends; move to nurture | Send a 6th "just bumping this" message |

### 0.3 What an early-stage outreach funnel realistically returns

Set expectations with these bands so nobody panics at message 200. These are honest early-stage figures, not vendor-pitch numbers:

| Channel | Positive-reply rate | Reply → Discovery call | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cold LinkedIn** | 1–5% | 30–45% | Higher if trigger is real and recent; collapses if generic |
| **Cold Email** | 1–4% (positive), 8–15% open-to-reply | 25–40% | Deliverability and trigger relevance dominate |
| **Warm LinkedIn** (1st-degree, dormant) | 15–30% | 40–55% | Existing relationship does the heavy lifting |
| **Referral Introduction** (double opt-in) | 30–50% | 50–70% | The highest-yield motion; protect the referrer |
| **Former Colleague** | 30–60% | 50–70% | Trust is pre-loaded; tone matters most |
| **Executive Outreach** (founder → CFO) | 5–12% | 35–50% | Peer-to-peer framing; brevity is mandatory |

Discovery → pilot conversion runs **20–35%**; pilot → paid runs **40–60%** for design partners selected (not accepted) per `03-design-partner-program.md`. Plan volume backward from there: to land 3–5 design partners you are realistically working a named list of 60–100 ICP-fit accounts.

### 0.4 The honesty guardrail (read before you customize)

Match the three-state discipline from `../delivery/08-design-partner-program.md`. In outreach you may reference **Live today** freely, you may name **Roadmap** items only as roadmap a partner can influence, and you may never imply target-state is live.

| State | Safe to claim in outreach | Example phrasing |
|---|---|---|
| **Live today** | Yes, directly | "ingests your CSV/Excel and Databricks data and answers finance questions with cited sources" |
| **Roadmap (staged)** | Only as influenceable roadmap | "native QuickBooks / NetSuite / Workday connectors are on the roadmap — design partners rank the build order" |
| **Target-state** | Never as present-tense fact | Do not say "fully pen-tested and SOC 2 certified." Say "certified Ready for Design Partners; full commercial security rating pending a live penetration test" |

### 0.5 Buying triggers — your `[TRIGGER]` field, sourced

Never send a message without a real trigger. These are the watch-list events (detect via LinkedIn job changes, news, funding databases, ERP/HRIS partner announcements, 8-Ks, earnings calls):

- **New CFO / VP Finance / Head of FP&A in seat <12 months** (highest-yield single trigger)
- **Fundraise** (Series B+ or growth round → board wants forecast rigor)
- **ERP or HRIS migration** (NetSuite, Workday, SAP cutover → data is in motion)
- **Audit, restatement, or material weakness disclosure**
- **Board pressure on forecast accuracy** (missed guidance, re-forecast announced)
- **FP&A hiring freeze or finance headcount cut** (do more with fewer analysts)
- **M&A / integration** (two charts of accounts, two budgets, no single source of truth)
- **Public commitment to "AI in finance"** (CFO LinkedIn post, conference talk, earnings remark)

## 1. Sequence A — Cold LinkedIn

**Use when:** No relationship, no mutual, but a clear `[TRIGGER]` and a strong ICP/persona match (`01-`, `02-`). Best for CFO, VP Finance, Head of FP&A, VP FP&A at companies in the ICP revenue band ($50M–$1B, ~200–3,000 FTE).
**Channel mechanics:** Connection request with a note (LinkedIn caps the note at 300 characters on most accounts — keep it tight). Then message after accept.

### 1.1 Cadence

| Step | Day | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Connect note | Day 0 | Send connection request **with** a short note (Message 1) |
| Message 2 | Day 2–3 after accept | First DM once connected |
| Message 3 | Day 6–7 after Message 2 | Value-add nudge, then stop |
| Move to nurture | Day 14 | If no reply, like/comment on their content; re-approach at next trigger |

### 1.2 Message 1 — Connection note (≤300 characters)

> Hi [FIRST NAME] — saw [TRIGGER, e.g., you stepped into the CFO seat at [COMPANY] this spring]. I'm building a finance-intelligence tool that answers questions like "why did [COMPANY]'s opex variance move" with cited data, not another dashboard. Would value comparing notes with someone in the seat. Open to connecting?

### 1.3 Message 2 — First DM after accept

> Thanks for connecting, [FIRST NAME].
>
> Quick context, no pitch: at a company [COMPANY]'s size, the first 90 days in a new finance seat usually means stitching forecast, actuals, and headcount out of spreadsheets before you can answer a single board question with confidence.
>
> We built Nexora to skip that — you ask a finance question, it retrieves the relevant data and returns a cited answer, like an FP&A analyst would.
>
> We're picking a small group of design partners to shape it. Worth a 20-minute conversation to see if your priorities line up?

### 1.4 Message 3 — Value-add nudge (then stop)

> Last note from me, [FIRST NAME] — I'll get out of your inbox.
>
> One thing design partners tell us lands fastest: budget-vs-actuals variance explained in plain English with the source rows cited, instead of a PivotTable they have to defend to the board.
>
> If forecast accuracy or variance commentary is on your list this quarter, I'd genuinely value 20 minutes. If the timing's wrong, no problem — I'll follow your work and reach out if something changes.

### 1.5 Do / Don't for Sequence A

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Make the connection note about *them and the trigger*, not you | Open with "I'd love to add you to my network" |
| Concede "no pitch" and mean it in Message 2 | Send a calendar link in the first DM |
| Cite a concrete output (variance explained, cited rows) | List the 8 agents or recite the tech stack |
| Stop cleanly at Message 3 and shift to social nurture | Send "just following up" four more times |

## 2. Sequence B — Warm LinkedIn

**Use when:** You are already 1st-degree connected (former network, conference contact, dormant relationship) but have no active conversation. The relationship is the asset — use it directly.

### 2.1 Cadence

| Step | Day | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Message 1 | Day 0 | Reconnect + reason for reaching out now |
| Message 2 | Day 3–4 | Frame the design-partner invite |
| Message 3 | Day 8 | Soft close + easy out |
| Move to nurture | Day 14 | Engage on their posts; re-approach at trigger |

### 2.2 Message 1 — Reconnect

> Hey [FIRST NAME] — it's been a while since [WHERE/HOW YOU KNOW EACH OTHER, e.g., the [EVENT] panel last year]. I've since started something I think is squarely in your world.
>
> I'm building Nexora — a finance-intelligence platform that behaves like an analyst: you ask it a question, it pulls the relevant data and answers with citations. Been thinking about you because [TRIGGER / WHY THEM].
>
> How have things been on your side at [COMPANY]?

### 2.3 Message 2 — The invite

> Appreciate the reply, [FIRST NAME].
>
> Here's why I reached out specifically: we're choosing a handful of finance teams as design partners — they get founder-level access, direct influence over what we build next (the connector roadmap, the agent behavior), and preferential terms ahead of general availability.
>
> Given [TRIGGER / their forecast or variance pain], I'd love your read on whether this fits [COMPANY] — even if the honest answer is "not now." Can I walk you through it for 25 minutes next week?

### 2.4 Message 3 — Soft close

> No pressure either way, [FIRST NAME] — I know the calendar is brutal.
>
> If it's easier, I can send a one-page overview and you tell me if it's worth a conversation. And if this isn't your priority right now, totally fair — I'd still value staying in touch.
>
> Either way, good to be back in contact.

### 2.5 Do / Don't for Sequence B

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Acknowledge the time gap honestly | Pretend you've been close all along |
| Ask a real question about *their* world first | Jump straight to the ask in Message 1 |
| Offer the one-pager as a low-friction alternative | Make them feel the relationship was only a pretext |
| Keep "not now" a genuinely acceptable answer | Guilt-trip the dormant connection |

## 3. Sequence C — Email

**Use when:** You have a verified business email and either a trigger or a weak warm thread (event lead, downloaded content, referral you couldn't route through LinkedIn). Email allows slightly more room and a subject line that does real work.
**Deliverability note:** Plain text, no images, one link maximum, no attachment on Message 1. Personalize the first line so it doesn't read as a blast.

### 3.1 Cadence

| Step | Day | Subject line | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Message 1 | Day 0 | `[COMPANY] forecast + a question` | Trigger-led intro |
| Message 2 | Day 4 | `re: [COMPANY] forecast + a question` | Reply-in-thread, add proof |
| Message 3 | Day 9 | `re: [COMPANY] forecast + a question` | Break-up / final value |
| Nurture | Day 21+ | New thread | Re-approach at next trigger only |

### 3.2 Message 1 — Trigger-led intro

> **Subject:** [COMPANY] forecast + a question
>
> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> Saw [TRIGGER — e.g., [COMPANY]'s Series C in March; the NetSuite migration you mentioned at [EVENT]]. Both usually put the same pressure on finance: the board wants tighter forecast accuracy and faster variance answers, and the data is spread across more spreadsheets than ever.
>
> I'm the founder of Sin City Analytics. We built Nexora to answer finance questions directly — you ask "what drove the Q2 opex variance," it retrieves the relevant actuals and budget and returns a cited answer, the way an FP&A analyst would. It stands up on your existing CSV/Excel and Databricks data — configuration, not a six-month implementation.
>
> We're selecting a few design partners to shape it before GA. Would a 25-minute conversation be worth your time — even just to pressure-test whether this is real?
>
> Best,
> [FOUNDER NAME]
> Founder, Sin City Analytics
> [PHONE] · [CALENDAR LINK]

### 3.3 Message 2 — Reply-in-thread, add proof

> **Subject:** re: [COMPANY] forecast + a question
>
> Hi [FIRST NAME] — following my note above.
>
> One reason I think this fits [COMPANY] right now: design partners are using it for board-ready variance commentary and rolling forecasts (3+9, 6+6, 9+3) without adding FP&A headcount. Every number comes with its source cited, and the agents are guardrailed to flag low-confidence data instead of guessing — which is the part finance leaders test us hardest on.
>
> To be straight about where we are: we're certified "Ready for Design Partners" — code and build certified, full commercial security rating pending a live penetration test. I'd rather you know that up front.
>
> Worth 25 minutes? Here's my calendar: [CALENDAR LINK].

### 3.4 Message 3 — Break-up / final value

> **Subject:** re: [COMPANY] forecast + a question
>
> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> I'll assume the timing isn't right and stop here — no hard feelings.
>
> If it's useful regardless, I'm happy to send our one-page design-partner overview: what partners get (founder access, roadmap influence, preferential terms), what we ask in return (your real data, candid feedback, a reference if it works). Just reply "send it" and it's yours.
>
> And if a forecast cycle or a board ask makes this urgent later, my door's open.
>
> Best,
> [FOUNDER NAME]

### 3.5 Do / Don't for Sequence C

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Keep Messages 2–3 in the same thread (`re:`) | Start a fresh subject each time |
| State the certification status honestly in Message 2 | Imply you're fully SOC 2 / pen-tested |
| Offer the one-pager as the only "asset," and only on request | Attach a 20-slide deck to Message 1 |
| Use one link max and plain text | Add tracking pixels, images, or three CTAs |

## 4. Sequence D — Referral Introduction

**Use when:** A mutual contact (`[MUTUAL]`) has agreed to introduce you. This is the highest-yield motion (30–50% positive, 50–70% to call). Protect the referrer's credibility above your own conversion.
**Mechanics:** Always run a **double opt-in** — the referrer asks the prospect's permission *before* connecting you. You supply the referrer a forwardable blurb so they barely have to write anything.

### 4.1 Cadence

| Step | Day | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Message 1 | Day 0 | To `[MUTUAL]`: the forwardable ask + blurb |
| (Referrer forwards) | Day 0–2 | `[MUTUAL]` requests permission, then intros |
| Message 2 | Day 0 (same hour as intro) | Your reply-all to the intro thread |
| Message 3 | Day 4 | Gentle nudge to the prospect if no reply |
| Thank referrer | Day 5 | Always close the loop with `[MUTUAL]`, outcome regardless |

### 4.2 Message 1 — The ask to your mutual (make it forwardable)

> Hi [MUTUAL],
>
> A small favor. You know [PROSPECT FIRST NAME] at [COMPANY] — would you be open to a quick intro? Given [TRIGGER — e.g., their new CFO / the funding round], I think Nexora could genuinely help, and I'd value their read either way.
>
> To make it zero effort, here's something you can forward verbatim:
>
> *"[PROSPECT FIRST NAME] — want to connect you with [FOUNDER NAME], founder of Sin City Analytics. They're building a finance-intelligence tool that answers finance questions with cited data (think FP&A analyst, not another dashboard). They're picking a few design partners and I immediately thought of you. Worth a short chat? Happy to intro if you're open."*
>
> Only if it's comfortable for you — no obligation at all. And thank you, either way.

### 4.3 Message 2 — Your reply on the intro thread

> Thanks for making the connection, [MUTUAL] — owe you one.
>
> [PROSPECT FIRST NAME], great to meet you. [MUTUAL] mentioned [SHARED CONTEXT / TRIGGER], which is exactly where Nexora tends to help: you ask a finance question — variance, forecast, vendor spend — and it returns a cited answer from your own data instead of sending you back to a spreadsheet.
>
> We're selecting a handful of design partners to shape the product before GA. I'd love 25 minutes to learn how [COMPANY] runs forecasting and variance today, and to let you judge whether this is worth your time. Would [DAY/TIME] or [DAY/TIME] work? Here's my calendar as a backup: [CALENDAR LINK].

### 4.4 Message 3 — Gentle nudge (only one)

> [PROSPECT FIRST NAME] — no rush at all, just floating this back up given how fast inboxes bury things.
>
> If a short conversation is worth it, grab any time here: [CALENDAR LINK]. If now's not the moment, just say so and I'll follow up down the road — and I won't keep [MUTUAL]'s intro hanging over you.

### 4.5 Do / Don't for Sequence D

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Run double opt-in; let `[MUTUAL]` get permission first | Cold-CC a prospect off a name-drop |
| Hand the referrer ready-to-forward copy | Make `[MUTUAL]` compose the intro themselves |
| Thank the referrer regardless of outcome | Go silent on `[MUTUAL]` after the intro |
| Limit yourself to exactly one nudge | Pester the prospect and burn the relationship |

## 5. Sequence E — Former Colleague

**Use when:** You worked directly with this person (prior employer, prior project, prior finance org). Trust is pre-loaded — your only job is to not abuse it. The tone is the most informal of any sequence; the ask is the most direct.
**Why it matters early:** Pre-PMF, your former colleagues are your fastest path to honest data, candid feedback, and a first logo. Several first design partners typically come from here.

### 5.1 Cadence

| Step | Day | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Message 1 | Day 0 | Genuine reconnect, no agenda |
| Message 2 | Day 3 | The honest ask: feedback first, partnership second |
| Message 3 | Day 8 | Easy out, keep it human |
| Nurture | Ongoing | This is a relationship, not a campaign |

### 5.2 Message 1 — Reconnect, no agenda

> [FIRST NAME]! It's been too long since [SHARED HISTORY — e.g., the [COMPANY/TEAM] days / the close-cycle marathons]. How are things at [CURRENT COMPANY]?
>
> I finally jumped — started my own thing, building a finance-intelligence platform. Would genuinely love to catch up and hear what you're wrestling with these days. Coffee or a call this month?

### 5.3 Message 2 — The honest ask

> So here's what I'm building, [FIRST NAME], and why I think of you specifically.
>
> Nexora answers finance questions directly — ask it about a variance, a forecast, vendor spend, headcount — and it pulls your data and answers with sources cited. You lived the pain of explaining a variance to leadership with three hours of spreadsheet archaeology; that's exactly what we're killing.
>
> Two things I'd value from you, in order: (1) brutally honest feedback on whether this is real, and (2) if it resonates, a conversation about [CURRENT COMPANY] being a design partner — founder access, you shape the roadmap, preferential terms. The feedback matters to me even if the partnership never happens. 30 minutes?

### 5.4 Message 3 — Easy out

> No worries at all if you're slammed, [FIRST NAME] — I know the rhythm.
>
> Standing offer: whenever you've got 20 minutes, I'd still love your gut read on what I'm building. You've always called it straight, which is exactly why I asked you first.
>
> Either way, let's actually catch up soon.

### 5.5 Do / Don't for Sequence E

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Reconnect as a person before pitching anything | Make the first message a thinly veiled sales note |
| Ask for *feedback first*, partnership second | Treat the friendship as a lead source only |
| Lean on shared, specific war stories | Use generic "we should reconnect" filler |
| Keep the relationship even if they say no | Disappear the moment they decline |

## 6. Sequence F — Executive Outreach

**Use when:** You (the founder) are reaching a **C-level or SVP economic buyer** directly — sitting CFO, COO, or a CEO at an ICP company — with no warm path. This is peer-to-peer: founder to executive. Brevity and respect for their time are the entire game. Longer than four sentences and you've lost.

### 6.1 Cadence

| Step | Day | Channel | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Message 1 | Day 0 | Email or LinkedIn | Peer-to-peer, one idea, one ask |
| Message 2 | Day 5 | Same channel | One proof point, re-ask |
| Message 3 | Day 12 | Same channel | Respectful close + standing offer |
| Nurture | Quarterly | — | Re-approach only at a fresh, material trigger |

### 6.2 Message 1 — Peer-to-peer

> **Subject:** Finance answers, cited — a 20-min founder-to-CFO conversation
>
> [FIRST NAME] — I'll be brief, peer to peer.
>
> I founded Sin City Analytics. We built Nexora so a CFO can ask a finance question — "what's driving the forecast miss," "where is vendor spend creeping" — and get a cited, defensible answer from your own data in minutes, not a deck a week later.
>
> Given [TRIGGER], I think there's a fit at [COMPANY]. I'm hand-selecting a few design partners and I'd value 20 minutes of your judgment. Worth a conversation?
>
> [FOUNDER NAME]

### 6.3 Message 2 — One proof point

> [FIRST NAME] — one concrete reason this is worth your 20 minutes.
>
> The design partners we're choosing run board-ready variance and rolling-forecast cycles without growing FP&A headcount, and every figure is traceable to source — the agents are built to flag uncertain data, not paper over it. That defensibility is usually what wins a CFO's trust or loses it.
>
> Straight version: we're certified Ready for Design Partners; full commercial security rating is pending a live pen test. I'd rather you hear that from me. Open to a short conversation? [CALENDAR LINK]

### 6.4 Message 3 — Respectful close

> [FIRST NAME] — I'll leave it here so I'm not noise in your inbox.
>
> If sharpening forecast accuracy or finance decision-speed climbs your priority list, I'd welcome the conversation any time — and I'm happy to start by simply sending what design partners commit to and get in return, no call required.
>
> Appreciate your time either way.
> [FOUNDER NAME]

### 6.5 Do / Don't for Sequence F

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Write founder-to-executive, as equals | Sound like an SDR working a quota |
| One idea, one ask, four sentences | Stack three value props and two CTAs |
| Name the trigger that makes *now* the moment | Send an undifferentiated "executive briefing" invite |
| Disclose the certification status plainly | Overstate security or imply enterprise scale you don't have |

## 8. Execution Discipline

### 8.1 Volume, tracking, and cadence hygiene

- **Weekly working set:** 15–25 net-new personalized touches per channel per week is sustainable solo; quality collapses past that. Better 15 sharp than 60 templated.
- **One trigger, one prospect, one message** — if you can't name the trigger, the prospect isn't ready; move them to nurture, not the send queue.
- **Track per account:** channel, trigger used, message step, reply sentiment, next action, next trigger date. Reply rates below the bands in 0.3 across 40+ sends mean the *trigger or targeting* is off, not the copy — fix upstream in `01-`/`04-`.
- **Stop means stop.** After Message 3, no more direct asks. Shift to social engagement and wait for a fresh, material trigger before re-approaching (minimum ~60 days).

### 8.2 Handoff to discovery

Every CTA in this document lands the prospect in the **discovery process** (`06-discovery-process.md`), qualified against `07-qualification-framework.md`. The outreach job is done the moment a conversation is booked — do not try to sell the platform in the thread. The first call's job is to listen, not demo.

### 8.3 The single failure mode to watch

The fastest way to torch this entire system is to send a "design partner" invite that reads like a discounted-software pitch. A design partner co-builds. If your copy ever implies "buy our product at a discount," you've broken the promise in `03-design-partner-program.md` and `../delivery/08-design-partner-program.md`. Re-read 0.1 and rewrite.
